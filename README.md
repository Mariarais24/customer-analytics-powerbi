## 📂 Data Source

Dataset provided by **Onyx Data / FP20 Analytics Challenge**. Covers 2024 customer feedback for OmniRetail — a U.S. retail chain selling electronics and smart home products across online and physical stores.

Data includes: satisfaction scores, purchasing behavior, demographics (age, gender), support history, loyalty levels, satisfaction factors, and geographic location.

---

## 💡 Dashboard Breakdown

### Business Problem
OmniRetail collected customer feedback throughout 2024 but lacked a structured way to diagnose what was actually driving satisfaction and loyalty — and where those experiences varied by region, demographic, or support interaction.

### Goal
Build an analytical report that uncovers the key factors influencing customer satisfaction and loyalty, surfaces regional and demographic patterns, and helps reshape OmniRetail's customer experience strategy.

### 🖥️ Walkthrough of Key Pages

---

**Page 1 — Customer Satisfaction Overview**

![Customer Satisfaction Overview](https://github.com/Mariarais24/customer-analytics-powerbi/blob/main/satisfaction-overview.png)

Top-level snapshot of satisfaction health. With an average score of 5.35 and 42% of customers dissatisfied, the dashboard immediately flags a retention risk. A satisfaction breakdown by age and gender reveals where the gaps are widest, a regional map surfaces geographic clusters, and a donut chart identifies the top 3 satisfaction drivers — Product Quality (36.55%), Packaging (33.91%), and Product Variety (29.55%).

**Page 2 — Customer Loyalty Insights**

![Customer Loyalty Insights](https://github.com/Mariarais24/customer-analytics-powerbi/blob/main/loyalty-insights.png)

Examines the relationship between loyalty level, satisfaction factors, and support contact. Only 30.83% of customers are loyal, while 57.50% are repeat buyers — a gap worth investigating. A stacked chart breaks down loyalty level by satisfaction factor, and a scatter plot maps loyalty clusters by region and satisfaction score.

**Page 3 — Customer Insights: Detailed View**

![Detailed View](https://github.com/Mariarais24/customer-analytics-powerbi/blob/main/detailed-view.png)

A drill-down diagnostic layer. Filterable by age group, location, and support contact status — allowing analysts to investigate individual customer records alongside location-level satisfaction scores. Houston, TX leads with an average score of 6.71, while several cities fall below the 5.0 threshold.

---

### Business Impact & Insights

- ⚠️ **42% of customers are dissatisfied** — the largest sentiment segment, signaling an urgent retention problem
- 🔁 **57.5% are repeat buyers but only 30.83% are loyal** — repeat purchase alone isn't translating to loyalty
- 📦 **Packaging ranks as the #2 satisfaction factor** — an often-overlooked lever for experience improvement
- 📞 **Customers who contacted support show lower loyalty (23% vs 38%)** — support experience may be eroding retention
- 📍 **Houston, TX leads satisfaction (6.71)** while other cities consistently underperform — points to regional inconsistency in experience delivery

---

## ✅ Recommendations

| # | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Investigate and address dissatisfaction drivers in underperforming cities |
| 2 | Improve support experience — contact with support correlates with lower loyalty |
| 3 | Use packaging and product variety improvements as quick satisfaction wins |
| 4 | Build a loyalty conversion strategy targeting repeat buyers who aren't yet loyal |
